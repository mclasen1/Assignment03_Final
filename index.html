In New York, Households in One Mid-Size County Spent the Most on Coffee Last Year

– Hint: It Wasn’t in New York City

By Maja Clasen | December 15, 2023



Nationwide, coffee soared in popularity in 2022. And it’s no secret that New Yorkers love their brew. Consumers spent almost $807 million on the drink that year, according to spending data.

But compared to everyone else in the state, households in Putnam County spent the most money per household on coffee – approximately $126.33 per year. Long Island’s Suffolk and Nassau counties followed closely behind at $122.62 and $122.45 per year, respectively.

While Putnam County residents only spent about $4.5 million in total on coffee – a measly sum when compared to the nearly $90 million spent in Brooklyn – Relative to their population size, the approximately 36,000 households here put more money towards the caffeinated drink than anywhere else in state.

Notably, the data found that the Bronx, Brooklyn, and Queens had the lowest per household spending on coffee.

It's not exactly clear why this one area has such high per-household spending on coffee in particular. But it does point towards an important factor: How much money households are able to devote to their favorite beverage.

Don’t be fooled by the small, hudson valley area. This county is one of the wealthiest in the state. With a median household income of over $120,970. Average residents here live on means well above the state average, and that of New York City dwellers.
